| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=436" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:16</a> | Why were shepherds an abomination to the Egyptians in Genesis? | Falk connected it both to Egyptian dislike of shepherding culture and to Israel's sacrifice of sheep and goats, which Egyptians generally did not sacrifice. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=534" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:54</a> | Could Joseph be identified with one of the viziers under the Hyksos rulers at Avaris? | He said no identification is presently possible because the records for Hyksos viziers are too sparse. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=675" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:15</a> | What effects did Babylon, Persia, Greece, and Rome each have on Egypt? | He said Babylon mainly raided, Persia fully conquered, the Greeks transformed the kingdom politically, and Rome ended Egyptian independence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=1096" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">18:16</a> | Is Deuteronomy 22:28 about rape or consensual premarital sex? | Falk said the passage addresses an unbetrothed pair discovered after sex and does not require the rape reading, unlike the clearly forceful case earlier in the chapter. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=1635" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:15</a> | Who are the five greatest Egyptian kings in his opinion? | He named several standouts from different periods, including Thutmose III, Djoser, Senusret III, Ramesses II, and Ramesses III. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=1930" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:10</a> | How much ancient Egyptian influence remains in Coptic Christianity, and is that blasphemous? | He said there is real cultural continuity in language, imagery, and some practices, and he did not regard that as blasphemous. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=2109" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:09</a> | In Joshua 8:26, is the weapon a curved sword like a khopesh, and what weapons were common then? | He leaned toward a curved bronze blade, probably khopesh-like, while noting that bows, slings, spears, and javelins were more common battlefield weapons. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=2576" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42:56</a> | Is James White a doctor in the same sense that Dr Pepper is a doctor? | Falk criticized inflated or purchased credentials and said such titles should not be claimed unless properly earned. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=2988" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">49:48</a> | Has he considered offering a class for people who want serious Egypt-and-Bible study without college? | Yes, but he said independent online teaching is hard to market and prepare well enough to justify the time. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=3233" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">53:53</a> | How does he respond to Afrocentrist claims that ancient Egyptians were simply black Africans? | He said Egyptian population history is mixed and gradient, with stronger African ancestry further south, and rejected sweeping revisionist claims. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=3518" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">58:38</a> | Was gold regarded as divine in ancient Egypt? | In a strong sense, yes: gold was associated with divine flesh and with funerary apotheosis. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=3750" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:02:30</a> | Does he think Moses wrote Genesis 1 to 2, or even Genesis 1 to 11 more broadly? | Yes. He argued Moses needed an authoritative counter-myth to unify a mixed multitude coming out of Egypt. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=4053" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:07:33</a> | Does he agree with claims of a long fourteenth-century BCE pandemic starting around Amenhotep III? | No. He said the clearer evidence for a major pandemic belongs later, around the Ramesside period. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=6539" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:48:59</a> | Was Potiphar a eunuch? | Probably not. Falk said eunuchs were rare in Egypt and that Potiphar's office as bodyguard does not require castration. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=6803" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:53:23</a> | How long was a "year" during Genesis 1 to 10 in early Israelite reckoning? | He said the best comparison is an early lunar calendar of roughly 354 days, though the exact system remains uncertain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CdHTkfIpCLQ?t=7129" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:58:49</a> | Is there historical evidence that Canaanite practices were corrupting Israel, such as child sacrifice? | Yes. He pointed to Egyptian and biblical evidence that at least some Canaanite groups practiced child sacrifice and related customs. |
