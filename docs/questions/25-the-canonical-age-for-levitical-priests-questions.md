| <a href="https://youtu.be/au5AYPIFPbY?t=1713" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28:33</a> | Did female virginity before marriage matter much in ancient Egypt? | Not in the way it did in ancient Israel. He described Egyptian sexual norms as comparatively permissive. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/au5AYPIFPbY?t=2182" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">36:22</a> | Is there really a contradiction over Moses's father-in-law being called Jethro or Reuel? | He said no. | <a href="https://youtu.be/au5AYPIFPbY?t=3146" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">52:26</a> | Is Genesis 10:10-11 referring to the Uruk expansion? | He regarded that identification as possible but too weakly evidenced to adopt confidently. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/au5AYPIFPbY?t=3402" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">56:42</a> | In what ways did Sumerian culture affect the writing of Genesis? | Very little directly by the time Genesis was written down, in his view, because Sumerian culture was already long gone except perhaps in distant landmarks and names. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/au5AYPIFPbY?t=3678" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:01:18</a> | How did snake charming work in the ancient Near East, and where did it come from? | He said it is attested across the Near East and seems to have worked broadly like later snake charming, with performance, handling, and family transmission of the craft. |
