# Questions in Livestream 16

Live Stream #16: Questions from the East

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=198" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:18</a> | Wouldn't Exodus 1:11 imply that Ramesses II was the pharaoh who enslaved Israel? | Not by itself. He argued Exodus 1 compresses actions from several reigns into one literary unit rather than describing a single king. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=557" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:17</a> | What can he say about the Therapeutae? | Only a little. He described them as an Alexandrian Jewish ascetic movement and said he had not worked on them in depth. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=728" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:08</a> | What advice does he have for someone trying to read and understand scripture properly? | Read at your own level, keep reading regularly, use real translations instead of paraphrases, and add good book-specific commentaries as needed. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=1339" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:19</a> | What does he think of the similarities between Psalm 104 and the Hymn to the Aten? | He saw real intertextuality, likely through shared cultural transmission, while still insisting Psalm 104 is its own Yahwistic text. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=1560" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">26:00</a> | What book does he recommend for learning biblical Hebrew? | He recommended Gary D. Pratico and Miles V. Van Pelt's kind of step-by-step classroom approach, praising the Kelly text he used later as clearer than older grammars. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=1957" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:37</a> | How common was child sacrifice in Canaan, and did it happen in Egypt? | He said Egypt offers little solid evidence, while Canaan has much stronger evidence and seems to have practiced it at least in some settings. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=2423" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40:23</a> | After God handed the nations over, could some divine beings have stayed loyal? | He rejected that Heiser-style framing and said the divine council should not be treated as a collection of rebel national gods. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=2553" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42:33</a> | How can we tell when an intertextual parallel is real rather than a misreading? | He said the key test is whether the texts address the same subject matter and whether the biblical text is borrowing, affirming, or criticizing that older material. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=2886" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:06</a> | Why did Lot offer his daughters to the crowd in Genesis 19? | He said it reflects the ancient Near Eastern duty of hospitality and protection, even though he also regarded Lot's action as morally troubling. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=3259" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">54:19</a> | Did Egyptians have real seafaring capability? | Yes. He said they traded across the Mediterranean and Red Sea and pointed to the Punt expeditions as clear evidence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=3510" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">58:30</a> | What does he think of Jordan Peterson and William Lane Craig? | He finds Peterson interesting but not yet fully committed, and sees Craig as a capable philosopher now working mostly at the lay-apologetics level. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=3934" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:34</a> | Would speaking to the rock in Numbers 20 be a form of stone worship? | No. He said it is not worship of the rock but a command over nature exercised through God's authority. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=4173" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:09:33</a> | Why do later biblical writers speak of Israel and Judah even when discussing the united monarchy? | He said this is a later editorial convention and retrojection used to make older material intelligible to later readers. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=5091" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:24:51</a> | What is *malamu*? | He explained it as the terrifying radiance or glory surrounding a god or king, something like the overwhelming trauma of holiness. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=5497" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:31:37</a> | Could the *Book of the Watchers* be older than commonly thought? | He did not know that text well enough to date it confidently, though he placed it within the broader flood of Second Temple literature. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=6845" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:54:05</a> | What does he think about the upcoming book on the route of the Exodus by Mark Janzen and Chris McKinny? | He called it an ambitious and worthwhile project, while warning that the route becomes much harder to verify beyond Sinai. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=7124" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:58:44</a> | What is the Egyptian word for rhinoceros? | He found an attested word and noted that the determinative shows a two-horned rhino, though the animal was rarely relevant in Egypt. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/8aV8S0cQ2Dg?t=7595" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:06:35</a> | Why doesn't it bother him that Judaism and Christianity borrow forms or ideas from surrounding cultures? | Because he sees that cultural grounding as part of how inspiration works: God redeems familiar forms rather than bypassing history and culture. |
