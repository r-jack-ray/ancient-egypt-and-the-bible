| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=695" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:35</a> | Did Satan fall before creation, or when he tempted Adam and Eve? | He said he places Satan's fall in connection with Adam and Eve rather than before the creation of the universe. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=776" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:56</a> | Does Matthew 7:18 deny that people can be complex or produce mixed results? | No. He read the passage as a warning about false teachers and ultimate fruit, not as a denial that human behavior can be mixed or complicated. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=1015" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:55</a> | What state would the Ark of the Covenant have been in by the time of Josiah? | Probably still serviceable. | <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=1526" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">25:26</a> | How should we understand Judges 1:8 given other texts and the archaeological questions around Jerusalem? | He treated it as compatible with a limited attack or burn episode without requiring full, permanent Judahite control at that moment. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=2368" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39:28</a> | How do you fit all the judges into the time between conquest and monarchy? | He said everyone has to reckon with overlapping judgeships and regional chronology, because simply adding all the years together does not work. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=2511" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41:51</a> | Is the Euphrates drying up in Revelation 16:12 literal? | Not necessarily. He treated it as possible but not required, since apocalyptic imagery often communicates more than flat literal prediction. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=2768" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">46:08</a> | How do you understand Jeremiah 3:16, especially with modern claims about rebuilding an ark? | He read Jeremiah as looking beyond reliance on the ark itself, toward a future where God's presence is no longer centered on that object. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=3318" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:18</a> | Why was Gezer targeted so often by invading armies? | Because of its strategic position in the Shephelah and the routes it controlled, not because it was uniquely symbolic on its own. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=3483" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">58:03</a> | When was Daniel written? | He leaned early rather than late, though without claiming absolute certainty, and at minimum placed it before the fourth century BC. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=3713" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:01:53</a> | Why is the late date for Daniel the consensus view? | He said the consensus is driven largely by scholars who reject predictive prophecy and therefore push Daniel down into the Maccabean period. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=4549" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:49</a> | What does `priest to the king` mean in 2 Samuel 20:26? | He took it as a court office, essentially a chief royal minister or high-ranking servant, not a second cultic priesthood. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=5151" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:25:51</a> | Who is Gog in Ezekiel: a human figure or a fallen angel? | He leaned toward a human or geopolitical ruler-symbol rather than a fallen angel, though he said the issue is debated. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=5449" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:30:49</a> | What is Rizpah doing in 2 Samuel 21:10? | He read it as a mourning and protest act tied to covenant violation, public shame, and the refusal to let the dead remain dishonored. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=7380" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:03:00</a> | What does Jesus mean in Luke 16:9 about making friends by means of mammon? | He said the point is faithful, shrewd stewardship: use worldly resources righteously rather than dishonestly, because small stewardship reveals larger fidelity. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=8402" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:20:02</a> | What happens to a nation's prince or spiritual power when that nation is politically defeated? | He said not much happens to the being itself; it simply continues under the next regime or political structure. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=9498" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:38:18</a> | Can Satan punish other spirit beings for disobeying him, and do they have to answer to him? | Probably he can punish, but they do not owe him true loyalty and do not simply function like a neat chain of command. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=10418" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:53:38</a> | What happens when a nation turns Christian? Do saints replace its spiritual rulers? | No. He said the principalities are not replaced by saints in that simple way, even when a society becomes Christianized. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwv2y8JJ7as?t=12476" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:27:56</a> | Why does the beast turn against the great prostitute in Revelation 17:16? | He read it as Satanic power eventually turning on the very false system it used, because evil has no real loyalty even to its own instruments. |
