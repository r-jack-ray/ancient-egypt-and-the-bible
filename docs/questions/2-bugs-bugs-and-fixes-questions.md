| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=136" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:16</a> | Did Akhenaten claim to be the son of the Aten, and did he accept worship from people? | Yes. He claimed to be the highest living manifestation of the Aten and accepted worship in that context; Falk describes Atenism as pantheism rather than monotheism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=304" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">5:04</a> | Was cutting off someone's nose a punishment in ancient Egypt, and why use that punishment? | Yes. It was a severe disfiguring punishment, sometimes used for serious public adultery cases instead of the death penalty. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=524" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:44</a> | Was pharaoh allowed to confiscate anyone's property for any reason? | Generally no. Pharaoh had prerogatives, but also had to obey long-standing judicial precedent and could not arbitrarily seize property, wives, or impose punishments without cause. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=657" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">10:57</a> | What do you think of the internal ramp theory of pyramid construction? | He is not persuaded by the internal ramp theory and favors external ramp explanations, especially work connected to Hatnub quarry evidence for moving large blocks up steep inclines. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=955" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">15:55</a> | Strong's has *haram* as "flat nose" in Leviticus 21:18. Is this a reference to cutting off the nose, and how does it relate to *herem*? | He is unfamiliar with that use for "flat nose" and cautions against relying on Strong's. In context, he takes the relevant idea as forbidden, disqualified, or consigned, not nose-cutting. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=1238" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">20:38</a> | If Israelites avoided idols, how could we rule out monotheism among them before Moses, and how does Judges fit? | He argues the evidence points against preserved Yahweh worship at Avaris; the Israelites likely worshiped other deities, while Moses re-encountered Yahweh in Midian. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=1659" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:39</a> | Do you have advice for Christians interested in studying Egyptology, ancient history, or Old Testament? | Choose the program by specialization, understand the career path, take the studies seriously, and treat scholarship itself as an act of worship. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=1912" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:52</a> | Is El also Yahweh? | It depends on context. *El* can mean a god generally, while Yahweh is a specific deity name; biblical *Elohim* can function as a magnificent plural for the ultimate God. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=2154" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:54</a> | Is it true that the people of Midian worshiped Yahweh? | He says some people in Midian probably did, though much of the support comes from the biblical text; the Shasu of Yhw evidence is suggestive but not certain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=2446" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40:46</a> | How would the events of Judges fit with a late date for the exodus, and was Jair in Judges the same Jair as in Numbers? | He answers the dating issue by saying Judges is not sequential but parallel and pedagogically arranged. He defers the Jair question because he has not studied it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=2656" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">44:16</a> | How ancient are the biblical sources for the Pentateuch? | He argues the major Pentateuchal sources point to a Late Bronze Age setting, roughly the 13th-12th centuries BC, based on names, vocabulary, geography, and Egyptian loanwords. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=2801" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">46:41</a> | What is your opinion of Inspiring Philosophy's case for prehistoric or very ancient monotheism? | He likes Inspiring Philosophy but is not convinced, mainly because he thinks the argument broadens monotheism to include henotheism or elevated-god polytheism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=2936" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:56</a> | What proof is there that Midian stretched into the Sinai Peninsula in Moses' time? | He points to Late Bronze Age ecology and ethnography: Sinai had pasture lands, Midian's range was broader, and Ishmaelites were present beyond Arabia. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=3123" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">52:03</a> | Who are considered Arab in the Old and New Testament? | The term shifts over time. He distinguishes ethnic Arab groups from the changing geographic label Arabia, which covered different regions in different periods. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=3270" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">54:30</a> | Have you read Tom Holland, and do you agree that religion as we define it did not exist before Christianity? | He has not read Holland's books and does not accept that claim unless "religion" is being defined in a special way; ancient Egyptian religion clearly involved gods and worship. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=3453" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">57:33</a> | How are the Sea Peoples and Philistines connected? | Philistines are one group within the broader Sea Peoples category. He thinks Philistine incursions into the coastal plain began before Ramesses III, likely by the late reign of Ramesses II. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=3680" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:01:20</a> | Is it possible to know who Abraham's pharaoh was? | No. Neferhotep I was contemporary with Abraham, but Abraham's Egyptian king could have been one of many short-reigning 13th Dynasty kings. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=3941" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:41</a> | Was Hammurabi retrojected into Abraham's time? | No. He thinks Abraham and Hammurabi were contemporaries, supported in part by comparable Elamite king-name evidence around Hammurabi's period. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=4100" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:08:20</a> | What do you think of Peter Feinman's view of the exodus? | He does not know Feinman's view, so he does not comment on it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=4132" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:08:52</a> | Do you know Gerard Gertoux? | Yes. He has tested Gertoux's chronology and says it failed for internal consistency, though he finds fringe chronologies interesting. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=4386" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:13:06</a> | Is Egyptian-style music today a real representation of ancient Egyptian music? | We do not know what ancient Egyptian music sounded like. Modern Egyptian or Arabic musical styles and instruments should not be assumed to preserve ancient Egyptian music. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=4527" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:27</a> | Can you recommend sources on ancient Nubia, Kerma, or Kush? | He cannot recommend a good popular source because Nubian studies is outside his specialty and there are few accessible works he knows. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=4702" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:18:22</a> | What are the biggest puzzles in biblical history that scholars should work on? | He highlights the exodus census numbers, reassessing Levantine archaeological reports in light of site-leveling discoveries, and major unresolved contextual issues around the flood narrative. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rm5VDYEkXz8?t=5244" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:27:24</a> | What are your thoughts on the pyramids, and who built them? | Kings built pyramids as royal tomb complexes or "resurrection machines." The workers were skilled craftsmen, not slaves, and evidence from Giza shows good food and medical care. |
