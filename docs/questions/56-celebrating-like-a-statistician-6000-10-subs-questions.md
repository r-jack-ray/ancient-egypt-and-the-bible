| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=553" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:13</a> | How should ghosts or disembodied human spirits be understood from a Judeo-Christian worldview? | He said "ghosts" covers several different phenomena, so no single explanation fits all cases; some reports may involve human spirits, but the subject is complex. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=1259" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">20:59</a> | If Egypt kept developing the cult of Ra, could it have become true monotheism? | He said no: at most it might have moved toward a henotheistic or pantheistic form, but not biblical-style monotheism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=1432" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">23:52</a> | What should pastors who may never become highly educated focus on studying? | He emphasized Hebrew, Greek, and serious critical commentaries, along with the discipline to prepare sermons carefully. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=2097" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">34:57</a> | Do passages like Zechariah 12:8 and Malachi 3:1 support reading the angel of the Lord as a christophany? | He said they may support a theophanic reading, but he would not claim they clearly prove a christophany. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=2398" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39:58</a> | If Egypt was the breadbasket of Rome, why did Egyptians so often face starvation? | He said famine pressures depended on how land was used and what rulers prioritized; agricultural abundance did not guarantee fair distribution. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=2671" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">44:31</a> | Were Egyptian charioteers mainly a noble hereditary warrior class? | Not necessarily. He said wealth mattered more than noble birth because someone needed the resources to own chariot equipment. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=2877" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:57</a> | What is the significance of Horeb as the mountain of God, and did it already have sacred status before Moses? | He said the text clearly treats it as sacred space, but any earlier religious significance remains uncertain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=3196" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">53:16</a> | Is Cain and Abel really derived from Seth, Osiris, and Horus? | He said no, calling that a mythicist overreach built on vague similarities rather than serious textual comparison. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=4333" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:12:13</a> | How can a God of truth use a deceiving spirit? | He answered in terms of primary and secondary causes: God remains sovereign, while the deceiving action is carried out by another agent under divine permission. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=4809" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:20:09</a> | In Revelation 1:6, are "kings and priests" and "kingdom and priests" equivalent? | He said no and preferred "kingdom and priests," since that reading fits the stronger textual evidence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=5577" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:32:57</a> | What are the pros and cons of the Septuagint and the Masoretic Text? | He said the Septuagint preserves an older translation stream and is valuable for textual criticism, while the Masoretic tradition is later but remains the main Hebrew base text. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=6142" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:42:22</a> | Did Abraham and Isaac really fear kings would kill them for their wives, or were they exploiting the situation? | He said the texts do not let us assign motive confidently, though ancient kings could behave like mob bosses, so the fear is plausible. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=6932" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:55:32</a> | Was Egyptian necromancy outlawed, and how did Egyptians seek knowledge from the dead? | No. He said contact with the dead was normal in Egypt and often expected to happen through dreams and funerary practice. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=7388" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:03:08</a> | In Daniel 9:27, is the one who stops sacrifice the Messiah? | He said no; the actions in that verse fit a wicked desolator, not Jesus. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=7637" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:07:17</a> | Where did the Egyptian gods come from? | He said Egypt's pantheon developed through a mix of local cults, deified forces of nature, and long historical layering rather than one simple origin. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=8242" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:17:22</a> | What were the Egyptian pyramids used for? | He said they were royal burials and resurrection monuments, with later secondary burials sometimes added around them. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=8601" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:23:21</a> | Why did Egyptians seem "allergic to shirts"? | He said the main answer is climate and dress practicality: Egypt was hot, and upper-body garments were often unnecessary or inconvenient. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=8758" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:25:58</a> | Did ancient Israelites believe that speaking positively or negatively could make things happen? | He said no in the modern manifestation sense; Proverbs about the tongue are about the moral and social power of speech, not magic formulas. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MA8Ualgbc4o?t=9808" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:43:28</a> | What are some up-to-date works on Proto-Sinaitic inscriptions? | He recommended Hamilton as the most current major work he had in mind, much newer than older standards like Albright or Gardiner. |
