# Questions in Livestream 69

Live Stream #69: They Might Be Giants.

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=600" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">10:00</a> | Did Abraham likely use images in worship, and are there ancient Near Eastern examples of worship without images? | He said Abraham probably did use images, since that was normal for the period, while truly imageless worship does appear only sparsely, with a few unusual Egyptian examples like offerings before empty shrines or thrones. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=859" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:19</a> | What is the third heaven like metaphysically, and are space and time there only conceptual? | He said heaven still seems to involve causality, sequence, and a kind of space, but not under the same framework as ordinary physical space-time. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=1034" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:14</a> | Do Judas Iscariot and Cain play central roles in real modern vampire-cult mythology? | He said Judas does not, but Cain sometimes does, especially through interpretations of the mark of Cain as a vampiric sign in some modern occult or vampire-religion circles. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=1631" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:11</a> | Is there proof in Genesis that the serpent is Satan? | Not in Genesis by itself. He said the identification comes from later biblical passages, especially Revelation 12:9, with Ezekiel 28 sometimes discussed alongside that theme. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=1881" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:21</a> | Were the Psalms always divided into five books, or is that a Masoretic arrangement? | He said the five-book division looks Masoretic, and the Dead Sea Scroll evidence suggests earlier psalm collections were more fluid. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=2467" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41:07</a> | If Sheol is not a place of punishment, why is the rich man suffering in Luke 16? | He distinguished torment from final punishment and treated the passage as describing agony in Hades rather than the last judgment itself. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=2760" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">46:00</a> | Does Proverbs 8 support the Trinity, and is Wisdom there Jesus despite being personified as feminine? | He said no. In his reading, Proverbs 8 personifies wisdom as an idealized woman rather than presenting a literal divine person of the Trinity. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=3490" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">58:10</a> | Did Yeshua meet Peter before the miracle of the fish in John 1:35-42? | He leaned yes from John's sequence, but said he had not studied closely enough to be fully certain how it aligns with the fishing-miracle account. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=3814" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:03:34</a> | Is the rapture biblical? | He said the idea is biblical in the broad sense that it comes from biblical texts, but he rejected the standard pre-tribulational Left Behind style system as resting on too many assumptions. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=4226" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:10:26</a> | Does salvation make Abraham's bosom obsolete, and can people still go there when they die? | He said Abraham's bosom no longer serves a purpose after Christ's redemptive work, so believers now go directly to heaven rather than to that intermediate compartment. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=5345" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:29:05</a> | What does Proverbs 3:5-6 really mean? | He said it does not forbid understanding or reason; it warns against relying on one's own understanding as ultimate instead of trusting God. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=6312" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:45:12</a> | Could the Cave of the Patriarchs or the traditional tomb of Aaron be evidence for Abraham or Aaron? | He dismissed Aaron's tomb tradition as highly doubtful and treated Abraham's cave only as weak circumstantial evidence supported by tradition and broadly fitting archaeology. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=6528" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:48:48</a> | If kosher laws no longer bind Christians, how should Isaiah 66:15-17 be understood when it condemns eating swine and mice? | He read the passage as judgment on idolatrous rebellion rather than as proof that new-covenant believers remain under kosher law. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=7769" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:09:29</a> | What does he think of identifying Babel with Eridu in Iraq? | He was skeptical and said the Eridu proposal depends on exceptionally thin and fragmentary evidence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=8306" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:18:26</a> | Why do some scholars reject the idea that the Hyksos were ancestors of the early Israelites? | He said Hyksos rulers and the later Israelites should not simply be equated: both relate to Semitic populations at Avaris, but they were not the same group and `Israelites` is itself anachronistic before the Exodus. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/gRDrvTEmitw?t=11547" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:12:27</a> | Is it worth studying Paleo-Hebrew or early alphabetic inscriptions? | Yes. He strongly encouraged it, arguing that Northwest Semitic epigraphy is valuable background for biblical study and badly needs more qualified specialists. |
