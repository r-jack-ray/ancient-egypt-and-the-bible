|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=244" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">4:04</a> | Why did bulls become such important sacrifices in the ancient Near East? | He said cattle were economically central, so sacrificing a bull meant surrendering something genuinely valuable, which made it a fitting major offering. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=395" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">6:35</a> | How should claims about Canaanites in Egypt and Hoffmeier's motives be evaluated? | He said motive attacks are a distraction. The real issue is the historical connection between Canaanites in Avaris, the Exodus tradition, and later Israel in Canaan. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=712" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:52</a> | Does Jephthah's "300 years" statement in Judges 11 help date the Exodus? | He said not much. Because Jephthah is presented as an unreliable speaker in that passage, the number is weak evidence for Exodus chronology. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=1166" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">19:26</a> | What are his thoughts on Sargon of Akkad? | He rejected the idea that Moses is copied from Sargon, but he said Sargon's story is important for understanding ancient river-culture child abandonment traditions and Near Eastern archetypes of the powerful usurper. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=1332" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:12</a> | Does he favor the short-sojourn or long-sojourn reading of the 430 years in Exodus 12:40? | He favored the short-sojourn reading, arguing that the Septuagint and Samaritan Pentateuch support counting time in both Canaan and Egypt. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=1464" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">24:24</a> | Could Chedorlaomer be identified with a known historical king? | Possibly, but he said the identification is uncertain because more than one candidate name fits the relevant window. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=1559" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">25:59</a> | How should Exodus 21 on slaves, beatings, and "property" be understood? | He said the passage still reflects slavery, but also that Israelite law placed unusual limits on slaveowners compared with surrounding cultures and should not be flattened into later chattel-slavery assumptions. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=1910" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:50</a> | What stands out about the Ain Dara temple? | He said the most memorable feature is the giant footprints at the entrance, a symbolic way of showing the deity entering the temple. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/CG3UQpEmAsk?t=2043" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">34:03</a> | Did Solomon repent before his death? | He said scripture is not explicit, so certainty is impossible, but he leaned toward seeing Solomon as still among the righteous despite ending badly. |
