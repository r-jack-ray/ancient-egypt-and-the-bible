# Questions in Livestream 70

Live Stream #70: For those in the Know

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=373" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">6:13</a> | What was the family structure in ancient Egypt like, and was it similar to the modern nuclear family? | He said it was somewhat similar, but usually broader, with servants or slaves and sometimes elderly parents treated as part of the household. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=567" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:27</a> | What are declensions, and did Egyptian have them? | Yes. He said Egyptian nouns and related forms do show declensional patterns, though far less elaborately than languages like Greek. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=674" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:14</a> | What did `a land flowing with milk and honey` mean to early Israelites, and was it an Egyptian idiom? | He said the phrase does appear in Egyptian material, but in the Bible it points to pastoral abundance, especially goat's milk and wild honey, rather than settled agricultural security. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=870" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:30</a> | Which paranormal podcasts does he listen to or recommend? | He recommended shows such as Weird Darkness and Unexplained Encounters, mainly as broad paranormal or mystery listening rather than as academic sources. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=1142" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">19:02</a> | Can he recommend a good history of the mystery schools? | Not really. He said the field is too broad and fractured, and he does not know a single book that covers it well. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=1422" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">23:42</a> | Does the Pelasgian creation myth with Eurynome bruising Ophion's head create a problem for Genesis 3:15? | No. He said that version is a modern reconstruction by Robert Graves rather than an authentic ancient myth parallel. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=2842" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:22</a> | What alternatives to the documentary hypothesis exist for Pentateuch composition? | He mentioned supplementary and redactional models as the main competitors, while arguing that each may contain some truth without adequately displacing a fundamentally Mosaic core. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=3350" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:50</a> | What does he make of the recently found rare Ramesses II era burial in Israel? | He said it is intriguing but still too unpublished to assess closely; for now it looks Egyptian-period and probably Canaanite rather than Israelite in character. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=3610" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:00:10</a> | What happens if someone blasphemes the Holy Spirit? | He treated it as a very specific sin tied to accusing Jesus directly of demonic possession, making it largely a sin of Christ's historical setting rather than a common modern occurrence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=3901" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:01</a> | What does he think about the Shroud of Turin? | He was doubtful, citing weak provenance and the tension between the one-piece shroud and biblical passages that seem to describe separate body and face cloths. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=4543" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:43</a> | Are there examples of pre-modern knowledge that seem surprisingly close to modern science? | He said some ancient thinkers made impressive observations, but most alleged examples are controversial because the ancient and modern ideas often are not really the same thing. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=6513" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:48:33</a> | Is there significance to the lack of a definite article before the numbered days in Genesis 1? | He did not think the missing article proves much either way; at most it shows the days are being sequentially framed, not whether they must be literal 24-hour periods. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=7136" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:58:56</a> | After the Merneptah Stele, what is the next historical reference to Israel? | He said the next clear reference comes much later, probably the Mesha Stele in the ninth century BC. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=7482" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:04:42</a> | Does he think Isaiah 40-66 was written by Isaiah or by later disciples? | He favored Isaiah himself, arguing that the book's large-scale reflective structure looks intentional even if the material may have been written and arranged over time. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=8792" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:26:32</a> | What Old Testament scene would he most like to witness in person? | He chose Abraham's encounter with the Egyptian king, wanting to see the court setting, the ruler involved, and the details the text leaves unstated. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=10822" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:00:22</a> | If Revelation 19 is Christ's second coming and the white-throne judgment follows a thousand years later, does that imply a third coming? | He said no; he treated the thousand years as an idiomatic period within the reign inaugurated by the second coming rather than as requiring a separate third return. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/3X9HQiOdJOk?t=11369" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:09:29</a> | Was music involved in courting Mrs. Falk? | No. He said their shared connection was more through reading and science fiction than through music. |
