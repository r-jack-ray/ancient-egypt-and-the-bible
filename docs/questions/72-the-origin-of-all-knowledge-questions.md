# Questions in Livestream 72

Live Stream #72: The Origin of All Knowledge

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=763" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:43</a> | Is there an etymological connection between the Egyptian god Atum and Adam, and can `atum` mean red? | No. He said the words are not etymologically related, and Egyptian `tm` refers to totality or completeness, not redness. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=1125" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">18:45</a> | Did ancient Egyptian omit the copular verb, and what about Greek? | He said Middle Egyptian often omits the copula, while later Egyptian uses it more regularly; Koine Greek, by contrast, normally requires a verb. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=1381" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">23:01</a> | How does Zoroastrianism fit into the development of gnosticism? | He did not think Zoroastrianism directly explains gnosticism and argued that gnosticism is not truly simple dualism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=2158" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:58</a> | Who are the ministering women with the mirrors in Exodus 38:8, and are the mirrors tied to Hathor worship? | He said the women are not fully identified in the text, but Egyptian mirrors commonly did carry Hathor imagery, so a Hathoric association is plausible. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=2618" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">43:38</a> | Could the Merneptah Stele refer to Israel still in Egypt or to Israelite tribes already in Canaan before the Exodus? | He rejected both options, arguing that the stele works better as a reference to Israel in Canaan after a real Exodus. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=3428" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">57:08</a> | Could Mesopotamian stories have been imported into Egypt during the Late Bronze Age? | Yes. He said texts and ideas moved widely across the ancient Near East, so such transmission into Egypt is entirely conceivable. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=5884" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:38:04</a> | Is the Book of Jonah satire? | He said no, though he acknowledged strong irony and humor in the book. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=8184" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:16:24</a> | Was `Malachi` a title like `my messenger`, or was it a personal name? | He said either is possible and the evidence is not firm enough to decide. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=9254" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:34:14</a> | Could the `Yabin` in Ramesses II's topographical list be the Jabin of Hazor? | Possibly, but he said he would need closer work on the list and the etymology before speaking confidently. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=9961" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:46:01</a> | Was manna real, and if so what might it have been? | He said yes, and suggested a fungus or mushroom is a more plausible explanation than tree sap. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=10477" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:54:37</a> | Why did Egypt not create overseas colonies like Phoenicia? | He said Egypt generally did not need them for trade, though he noted Byblos as an exceptional Egyptian colonial outpost. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/d837zh26DZw?t=11007" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:03:27</a> | What is a good overview book on Egyptian mythology and its development? | He recommended Wilkinson's *Gods and Goddesses of Ancient Egypt*. |
