# Questions in Livestream 23

Live Stream #23: The 23 Enigma

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=257" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">4:17</a> | If Egypt influenced the Old Testament so much, why is so much more focus placed on Mesopotamia? | He said the imbalance mostly comes from academic tradition and language training, since Mesopotamian studies are easier to add onto Hebrew-based biblical study than Egyptology is. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=445" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:25</a> | What does he know about the Egyptian papyri Joseph Smith owned and "translated"? | He said the surviving papyri are ordinary funerary texts from the Book of the Dead tradition, not Abraham's writings. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=663" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:03</a> | Is there evidence that Ramesses II lost his firstborn son and that Egyptian activity in the Levant declined around the Exodus? | Yes to both in a broad sense. He dated the firstborn's death to roughly Ramesses II's year 25 and said Egyptian hegemony later waned. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=837" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">13:57</a> | What does he think of the Talpiot tomb claim as Jesus's final resting place? | He rejected it, saying the names are too common and the case is statistically and methodologically weak. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=1006" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:46</a> | Why should Adam and his sons be read as clans or tribes when Genesis gives them a narrative? | He said Genesis is structured around clan politics, and the genealogies make better sense when the named fathers stand for clan lines rather than isolated individuals. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=1341" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:21</a> | Why is the abandonment of Avaris considered important evidence for the Exodus by some scholars? | Because the site shifts abruptly from settlement use to funerary use in the Ramesside period, raising the question of where its Levantine population went. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=1590" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">26:30</a> | Does he accept systems-collapse theory for the Bronze Age collapse, and were the Sea Peoples a cause or a symptom? | Partly. He said collapse unfolded over centuries, with the Sea Peoples acting as both symptom and contributing cause. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=1811" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">30:11</a> | Were the Nephilim a fallen priesthood, and what about the Anakim? | He said the Nephilim are better understood as the result of a fallen priesthood, while he deferred a fuller answer on the Anakim. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=1999" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33:19</a> | Is ancient idolatry basically like a genie-in-a-lamp view of trapping a god in an image? | Fairly close. He said idols were often treated as images meant to entice or host divine presence, and Genesis 1 reverses that logic by making humans God's image. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=2180" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">36:20</a> | Does Tubal-cain working iron argue against reading Genesis 4 in clan terms? | No. He said naming an individual craft founder still fits a clan-based reading because clans are named from their progenitors. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=2482" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41:22</a> | Should Behemoth and Leviathan be identified as a hippo and a crocodile, and how should archangels be viewed in ancient Near Eastern context? | He rejected a simple animal reading for Leviathan, treating it as a mythic sea monster and reading biblical angelic language through intertextual engagement with older imagery. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=2900" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:20</a> | Is there evidence of witchcraft, wizardry, or magic elsewhere in the ancient Near East the way scripture describes? | Yes, in abundance. He said Egypt, Mesopotamia, and the Levant are saturated with magical practice, with the key distinctions often being public versus private and legitimate versus illegitimate use. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=2942" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">49:02</a> | What is "abrech" in Genesis 41:43? Does it mean something like "kneel" or have an Egyptian origin? | He said the term is still unclear. An Egyptian etymology is possible, but he was not prepared to treat it as settled. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=3435" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">57:15</a> | Can a case be made that the flood was local while still functionally worldwide? | He thought a purely local reading runs into real problems, especially the ark's animal cargo, even if a limited-human-world perspective explains part of the story. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=4119" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:08:39</a> | Is the Potiphar story basically the same as the Tale of Two Brothers? | Only vaguely. He said there are some parallels and possible name echoes, but the legal and social settings are quite different. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=4420" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:13:40</a> | Was it normal in the ancient Near East to bury families together, and why was Leah buried with the patriarchs instead of Rachel? | Yes, family tombs were common. He said Leah's burial reflects her role in the ancestral family tomb structure. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=4804" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:20:04</a> | Did Ramesses II's firstborn son die from a head injury? | He said there is no evidence for that and no Egyptian record of the cause of death. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=4913" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:21:53</a> | Was the witch of Endor an anomaly? | No. He said the surrounding nations practiced magic openly, and Saul had to cross out of Israelite-controlled territory to seek her. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=5384" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:29:44</a> | Did the Pharaoh of the Exodus drown with his army, or only the army? | He said the text points to the destruction of the chariot force and army, not to Pharaoh himself drowning. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=5846" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:37:26</a> | Were pyramids famous around the world during the pyramid age itself? | Not nearly as early or as widely as people assume. He said they become clearly famous much later, especially from around 700 BCE onward. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=6143" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:42:23</a> | Did the name Jesus come from "hail Zeus"? | No. He called that an untenable pseudo-etymology and said the name is simply the Greek/Hellenized form of Yeshua. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/DVV7DMRiBL4?t=6704" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:51:44</a> | How should Christians read the church fathers and patristic literature? | He said they should be read in context as early witnesses after the apostles, not as proof-texts detached from their historical setting. |
