# Questions in Livestream 31

Live Stream #31: Twas the Night Before the Night Before Christmas.

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=211" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:31</a> | Is there a dating conflict in Genesis when Isaac deals with Abimelech king of the Philistines? | He said not necessarily. He argued that the "Philistines" language may reflect either an older Palaset group in that region or a broader retrojective label. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=710" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:50</a> | What study documents signs of hunger or starvation in ancient Egyptian mummies? | He cited Sandison and Tapp's work in *Mummies, Disease and Ancient Cultures* and pointed listeners to that scholarship. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=778" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:58</a> | Would mandatory logic courses improve biblical and historical scholarship? | He said yes, substantially. In his view, both fields often tolerate weak argumentation that better training in logic would expose. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=962" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:02</a> | What does he think of Jordan Peterson's Genesis lectures? | He found them interesting but mostly eisegetical, reading Genesis through a Jungian framework rather than treating it as strong biblical exegesis. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=1121" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">18:41</a> | Were the Gospels written from eyewitness testimony or mostly passed down orally first? | He leaned strongly toward eyewitness grounding, especially for John and Matthew, while allowing that Luke explicitly worked through informed sources. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=1344" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:24</a> | What evidence should we expect for a Davidic or Solomonic kingdom, and how big was it? | He said the evidence for both kingdoms already exists and that the Solomonic kingdom in particular appears to have been extensive. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=1563" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">26:03</a> | Who was Akhenaten, was Atenism related to Joseph or biblical monotheism, and why is Amarna art so strange? | He said Atenism was not biblical monotheism but closer to monolatry or pantheistic thinking, and the unusual art style reflects that broader religious program. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=1951" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:31</a> | What can he say about Migdal Eder, the shepherds, the manger, and the swaddling cloths? | He treated the shepherds as rugged field workers, the manger as a feeding trough, and the nativity setting as markedly humble rather than romanticized. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=4536" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:36</a> | Was Moses's basket left to chance on the Nile? | He said no. In his reading, the basket was set in a controlled place among the reeds, watched over, and not simply sent drifting away. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/TT2tApnGZPY?t=5413" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:30:13</a> | What was the star of Bethlehem? | He said a planetary conjunction is more likely than a purely miraculous light, with the magi interpreting it through existing astronomical omen traditions. |
