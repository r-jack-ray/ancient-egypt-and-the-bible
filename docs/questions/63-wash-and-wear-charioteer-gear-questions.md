# Questions in Livestream 63

Live Stream #63: Wash and Wear Charioteer Gear.

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=354" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">5:54</a> | How did the Egyptians view numbers: literally or symbolically, and did that influence the ages of the patriarchs in Genesis 5 and 11? | Egyptians used numbers in several ways, including literal, ordinal, and symbolic uses; he does not think that Egyptian number symbolism drove the patriarchal ages, which he reads as clan lifespans rather than single individuals. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=769" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:49</a> | How did Moses know the events of Genesis 1 through 11? | He says the material was handed down through earlier generations and then written down in the New Kingdom period, likely around the 13th century BC. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=896" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:56</a> | If the promised land border is the Brook of Egypt, what is its limit, and could it include the Sinai mines such as Serabit? | He identifies the brook with Wadi el-Arish and treats it as an exclusive boundary, so he does not think the border pushes far enough into Sinai to include Serabit. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=1059" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:39</a> | If Judges presents a decline from better to worse judges, why are Samson and Jephthah still included in Hebrews 11? | Hebrews 11 highlights particular acts of faith, not an entire life record, so flawed figures can still be cited for moments when they trusted God. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=1518" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">25:18</a> | Do you know any good books on Egypt under Roman rule? | He says he does not have a recommendation because Roman Egypt is not an area he studies with interest. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=1749" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">29:09</a> | If God knows we are going to sin, why not create people who would not sin, and why punish us for sinning? | He frames this as the problem of theodicy and argues that God made humanity with agency, while Christ's atonement means the central issue becomes whether one chooses God's side over sin. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=2137" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:37</a> | Is there a good overview book on the people groups mentioned in the Bible, such as Sheba, the Hittites, Babylon, and their cultures? | He says no single book covers all of that well, but suggests *The Making of the Middle Sea* as a broad primer that gets relatively close for many of those cultures. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=2419" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40:19</a> | What do you think about Ken Ham's Noah's Ark at the Ark Encounter theme park? | He sees it as entertainment built around a specific interpretation, and argues it has unresolved exegetical problems, especially around Egyptian chronology and the pyramids. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=2847" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:27</a> | What role do you think Solomon played in the writing of Ecclesiastes? | He thinks Solomonic authorship is plausible and probably high, but not absolutely provable from the text. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=3308" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:08</a> | What did the career of an Old Testament prophet actually look like? | He says many prophets mainly settled disputes and clarified present situations; some came through prophetic schools, while others were called prophets who still kept ordinary day jobs. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=3902" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:02</a> | In Numbers 31, Moses fights Midian even though his wife and in-laws were Midianites. Does that mean he betrayed his own family? | He says no; the Midianite leaders involved had already aligned against Israel, so the campaign is presented as retribution within a clan-conflict framework, not betrayal. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=4384" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:13:04</a> | How should someone respond to militant Messianic Jews who say Sabbath must be Saturday and that Sunday church is satanic? | He distinguishes different Messianic Jewish positions, argues this kind of claim is legalistic, and says Sabbath timing is not a salvific issue. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=5060" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:24:20</a> | Is a hot dog a sandwich? | He says no: a sandwich is two pieces of bread with filling between them, while a hot dog is a sliced bun with a sausage. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=5529" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:32:09</a> | How does the Old Testament inform church structure today, including priests, prophets, apostles, and related offices? | He says the Old Testament still informs church order and teaching responsibility, but many older offices are not carried over in the same way because Christ fulfilled the sacrificial system and changed the church's structure. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=6092" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:41:32</a> | Were the Ptolemies as debauched as the classical historians say? | He says they were certainly notorious drinkers, but he is less convinced by the more extreme claims and notes that they were not necessarily terrible rulers by Egyptian standards. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=6358" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:45:58</a> | You mentioned Hebrew was lost and recovered twice. Can you explain the circumstances? | He says Hebrew first ceased as a living language after the Roman dispersions and was revived in the Masoretic era, then later declined again before being revived as a spoken language in the modern Zionist period. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=6661" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:51:01</a> | In 2 Kings 2:23-24, is it possible that the 42 youths were threatening Elisha rather than merely taunting him? | Yes; he argues they were not small children but a threatening gang trying to force Elisha toward a cultic high place at Bethel. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=7120" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:58:40</a> | Would you consider the Ptolemaic dynasty the soap-opera period of ancient Egypt because of all the family drama? | He says yes, it was full of intrigue, co-rule tensions, and royal family drama, even if that is not the period he finds most interesting. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=7280" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:01:20</a> | Why does 1 Chronicles 7:20-24 place Ephraim after the exodus? | He explains this through clan identity: the later Ephraim in the passage is a descendant and clan leader bearing the ancestral clan name, not Joseph's son himself. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=7734" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:08:54</a> | Do you think Neferneferuaten was Nefertiti? | He says no; he currently leans toward Smenkhkare, while acknowledging that the evidence is incomplete and the question remains debated. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=8082" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:14:42</a> | If I want to study church history, where should I start? | He recommends starting with a general church history survey, then following the period that most interests you. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=8280" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:18:00</a> | If Jericho's final destruction is placed around 1350-1325 BC, how does that fit with a 13th-century exodus? | He argues the relevant destruction fits Late Bronze 2B, which better aligns Jericho with Hazor and therefore with a 13th-century exodus model. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=8971" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:29:31</a> | How interested were ancient Israelites in Mount Sinai after conquering the land? | He says not very; Sinai was not the promised land and only became a limited pilgrimage interest much later, especially under Roman and later Christian conditions. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=9386" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:36:26</a> | What punishments were there in Egypt for offending or insulting the king, and for other crimes? Are there any good resources on this? | He says punishments depended heavily on who was wronged: offenses against the king could mean execution, while lesser offenses could bring beatings or even no serious response. |
| <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6II72aUU4fM&t=9677" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:41:17</a> | Do you believe we are in the end times? | He says Christians have been in the end times since Christ's resurrection, but he does not claim to know whether the final stretch is immediately near. |
