Special Live Stream: "Reliquary of the Dead" Q&A

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=379" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">6:19</a> | Why are you releasing two books so close together? | He said they wanted book two close behind book one so readers were not left hanging and so the series would feel real and ongoing. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=484" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:04</a> | Why did you choose an open-ended series rather than limiting it to three or seven books? | He said they never intended only three books; it is an open world with more books already planned. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=622" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">10:22</a> | Is this a Christian novel? | Not in the usual market sense. He called it fiction that is Christian in worldview without being standard "Christian fiction." |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=711" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:51</a> | Does the novel use Christian allegory, symbols, or a conversion-and-baptism ending? | Not really. He said there is no baptism ending and the book does not work as overt Christian allegory. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=769" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:49</a> | Is the opening chapter autobiographical? | No. He said it was not autobiographical, though parts were inspired by Pierre Montet and real Egyptological career drama. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=945" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">15:45</a> | What does the line about a complete skeleton not fitting in one bag mean? | He said it reflects real osteology practice, where bones are separated into specimen bags and stored in boxes. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=1032" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:12</a> | What is the message or theme of the novel? | He said the core theme is that personal redemption takes tenacity. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=1222" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">20:22</a> | Why choose a novel rather than poetry or a play? | He said the novel best fit a large story that could teach and entertain without collapsing under the limits of poetry or theater. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=1646" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:26</a> | What was your favorite non-main character to write? | Melissa. He especially enjoyed the osteology-lab scene built partly from real experience. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=1876" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:16</a> | Who inspired Chin, and was he based on a real terrible person? | Yes. He said Chin was inspired by a real person he had known and strongly disliked. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=1983" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33:03</a> | Why does the Kindle edition not have a table of contents? | He said they simply did not realize Kindle readers expected that and plan to look into it for future books. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=2141" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:41</a> | What inspired the book? | He said he wanted a fiction vehicle that could communicate archaeology and the ancient world to a broader audience. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=2740" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">45:40</a> | Are weapons in the colony reused older Earth guns or more sci-fi weapons? | Updated conventional firearms. He said they use practical guns, not lasers or blasters. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=2834" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:14</a> | What was the writing-process timeline for the book? | He wrote the first three chapters in 2018, set it aside, then finished the rest in late 2024 over about three months. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=2969" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">49:29</a> | Is an Audible release possible? | Maybe eventually, but only if the economics improve enough to justify the cost. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=3937" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:37</a> | What gave you the idea that Glee 832C has no tectonic plates? | He drew on Mars and other planetary models and wanted Glee to be a super-Earth with a different geological profile from Earth. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=4368" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:12:48</a> | Will there be spoilers in this stream? | Yes. He said spoilers were unavoidable for a stream built around discussing the book openly. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=4652" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:17:32</a> | Are you a writing gardener or architect, and how do you make characters believable? | More gardener or discovery writer. He said he discovers the story as he writes and lets characters develop naturally. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=4796" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:19:56</a> | Do good stories need to subvert expectations and be original, or can classic tropes still work? | Classic tropes can still work well if handled skillfully. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=4942" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:22:22</a> | Did you always plan to end the book with a big cliffhanger? | Yes. He said he generally knows the ending shape and wanted that cliffhanger. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=5334" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:28:54</a> | If someone is starting a series, how many books should they plan at once as a limit? | He did not give a rigid rule, but urged writing enough to show readers the series is genuinely going somewhere. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=5994" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:39:54</a> | How many sales count as a novel doing really well? | He said the bar is lower than many readers assume, and breakout success is rare. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=6380" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:46:20</a> | Could humanity eventually put people into non-aging stasis for deep-space travel? | Probably yes, he said; he treated that as more plausible than faster-than-light travel. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=7282" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:01:22</a> | When are you going to do the obligatory ancient-alien fake-out and then debunk it in the novel? | He said he is not planning to do that. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=7382" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:03:02</a> | Why is the planet called Glee? | He said that is the astronomical name assigned to the planet. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=7901" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:11:41</a> | Could human consciousness ever be digitized and uploaded? | No. He treated it as a data-conversion problem that cannot really reproduce consciousness. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=8239" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:17:19</a> | What are some real-world examples of engrams? | He compared them to encoded information like a self-playing piano roll that stores actionable pattern data. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=8765" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:25:59</a> | Will the Outcasts play a bigger role in the future? | Yes. He confirmed they will matter more later, though he would not say how. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=8833" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:27:13</a> | Will we get at least one likable character in the next book? | Yes. He said there will be at least one especially likable character. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=8930" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:28:50</a> | Did an Indiana Jones death scene influence a certain death near the end? | No. He said Indiana Jones did not influence that part of the book. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=9983" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:46:23</a> | About how many hours does it take to read the book? | He did not give a firm time estimate, but noted the book is about 80,000 words. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/MXOn6JjytCE?t=10084" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:48:04</a> | Are you okay with *War of the Gods* fanfic or fan art being made? | Yes, with the caveat that people should respect what the books establish as canon. |
