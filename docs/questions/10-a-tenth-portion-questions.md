| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=262" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">4:22</a> | Which Egyptian king do you think was King Solomon's father-in-law? | Not Shoshenq I; he says Siamun or Psusennes II are the plausible candidates because the weakened late 21st Dynasty had reason to seek alliances. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=448" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:28</a> | Are you familiar with Ben-Yosef's theory that the lack of archaeological evidence for a united monarchy is because they were nomadic, and do archaeologists have an architectural bias? | He thinks the theory came too late to be persuasive because 10th-century Israelite cities are being found, but he does think the academy has a skeptical and architectural bias toward biblical evidence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=585" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:45</a> | Can you explain the connection between David's mighty man Abishai and the Hyksos leader Abba-shai in the tomb painting? | The shared name does not prove a direct connection; both are West Semitic names from related language contexts, and repeated names need place and context to identify people. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=766" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:46</a> | How do you interpret the Dead Sea Scrolls and Deuteronomy if El Elyon and Yahweh appear different? | From the Masoretic Text and Septuagint available to him, he does not see two deities; he treats El Elyon and Yahweh as different names for the same God, while noting the Dead Sea Scroll reading would need more study. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=1014" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:54</a> | Are there surviving busts of kings and queens besides the Nefertiti bust, and were they common? | Yes, several survive, especially from the New Kingdom and Amarna period, including Akhenaten and Tutankhamun examples, though he would not call them common. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=1202" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">20:02</a> | Did biblical archaeology's early success create an academic backlash? | He says the backlash came from both higher criticism and biblical archaeology's own methodological failures, especially confirmation bias, not simply from its success. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=1689" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28:09</a> | Do we know anything about the other kings besides Hammurabi and Amraphel in Genesis 14? | Very little. Some Elamite candidates have names close to the biblical form, but the other kingdoms are hard to locate, so identification remains uncertain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=1868" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:08</a> | What do you know about Egyptian astrological decans or faces, their stars, and their use? | He says Egyptian astrology is outside his specialty, but notes Egypt had its own constellations, temple and tomb star imagery, the Dendera zodiac, and some medical use tied to Sothis. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=1939" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:19</a> | What was the significance of the tenth plague? | He frames the death of the firstborn as an attack on Egypt's power structure and succession system, intended to shock and destabilize the country. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=2297" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">38:17</a> | Do we see a symbolic equivalent of an Egyptian frieze in ancient architecture before the Egyptian empire? | He says the earliest true uraeus frieze appears in Dynasty 3 at Djoser's mortuary temple, with earlier allusions in some early mastabas. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=2556" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42:36</a> | Is there a correlation between climate change, the Younger Dryas, and the birth of Egyptian civilization? | Climate change helped push settlement toward the Nile as the Sahara dried, but Nile communities and rudimentary agriculture already existed long before dynastic Egypt. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=2823" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:03</a> | Will you ever do fan mail? | Probably not in a reply-oriented form; he says emails and fan mail take time away from writing, publishing, and original research. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=2947" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">49:07</a> | Did the Hurrians and Mitanni leave any footprint on biblical tradition? | Yes, but a small one: he identifies biblical Hivites with Hurrian-related groups and notes some Hurrian vocabulary, while saying they did not leave a lasting large impression. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=3079" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">51:19</a> | Could you elaborate on Schneider's proposed etymology for Moses's name? | He finds Schneider's proposal compelling: Moses may come from an Egyptian `m-shay` style name, "of the lake," with the deity name removed. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=3313" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:13</a> | Why does Exodus 14:2 mention Baal-zephon, and was it merely an outlier geographical name? | He says it was not merely an outlier; Baal worship entered Egypt through Semitic groups at Avaris, was connected with Seth, and Baal-zephon also functions as a Ramesside-era geographical marker. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=3613" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:00:13</a> | Were Avaris buildings repurposed the way Pi-Ramesses stone blocks were reused at Tanis? | No. Avaris was mostly mudbrick, which was not reused like stone, while Pi-Ramesses had stone temples, pylons, and statuary that could be scavenged for Tanis. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=3779" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:02:59</a> | Did the Hittites of Hattusa leave a biblical footprint? | Yes. He points especially to the Neo-Hittite states after the Late Bronze Age collapse and references to the "kings of the Hittites" in Kings and Chronicles. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=4063" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:07:43</a> | Is Barry Beitzel right that the Red Sea event happened close to Egypt rather than hundreds of miles away? | He agrees. He places the Sea of Reeds near the Pelusiac estuary and rejects a Gulf of Aqaba location as unsupported by the Egyptian and biblical landmarks. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=4277" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:11:17</a> | Do you have a beginner recommendation for the Bible's historical backgrounds? | He recommends *Behind the Scenes of the Old Testament*, edited by Greer, Hilber, and Walton, as an approachable early-college-level introduction. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=4375" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:12:55</a> | What are the *tanninin* created on the fifth day: whales, sea monsters, or something else? | He thinks they are not simply whales; the term points to large sea creatures or sea monsters, but the exact referent is uncertain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=4534" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:34</a> | Where can someone find Egyptian texts in their original language, such as the Anastasi papyri? | He advises beginners to start with Middle Egyptian texts such as Sinuhe, Ipuwer, Neferti, or Middle Kingdom stelae before tackling late Egyptian texts like the Anastasi papyri. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=4674" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:17:54</a> | How do Proverbs 22-23 compare with the Instruction of Amenemope, and why would Solomon repurpose it? | He says Proverbs draws on shared wisdom literature because good common-sense moral instruction can be repurposed for Israel's covenant setting. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=5151" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:25:51</a> | How functional were Egyptian hand mirrors? | Very functional when new. He says polished bronze or metal mirrors could be highly reflective, even though surviving examples are usually tarnished. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/C79MxFcngZ4?t=5497" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:31:37</a> | If the conquest was hyperbolic in its total-destruction language, does the Benjaminite episode in Judges ruin that view? | He says the comparison does not really apply: conquest language has a range of meanings and the Benjaminite narrative is a later, separate episode about internal retribution and mob justice. |
