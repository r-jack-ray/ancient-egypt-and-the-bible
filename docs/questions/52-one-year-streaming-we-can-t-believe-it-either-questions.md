| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=368" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">6:08</a> | Why did God use circumcision as the sign of the covenant with Abraham, and is there an ancient Near Eastern context behind it? | He says the exact reason is not fully known, but covenant signs were normal in suzerain-vassal treaties and circumcision was an old practice in Egypt and the wider ANE. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=494" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:14</a> | Do you think Siamun is the pharaoh mentioned in 1 Kings 3:1? | Yes. | <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=669" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:09</a> | What was the Egyptian prison like for Joseph, and what did it look like if he was put in charge of other inmates? | He describes Egyptian prisons as open-air stone-quarry holding facilities, with prisoners working under guards while awaiting judgment rather than living in walled cells. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=1271" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">21:11</a> | Who preserved the story of Abraham and his clan until Moses, and was there a job for preserving oral tradition? | He says oral tradition did not require a special job; clan stories and genealogies were communally preserved, though Levites may have had a role. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=1565" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">26:05</a> | What is the history of Catholicism, and did popes change God's word, commands, or laws? | He recommends reading church historians widely and rejects the claim that popes directly rewrote Scripture, while distinguishing interpretation and translation issues from textual manipulation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=2005" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33:25</a> | Is Ur in Mesopotamia, and what about claims that the river moved? | He says it depends which Ur is meant, arguing that southern Ur is not between the rivers while a northern Ur such as Urkesh would better fit Mesopotamia. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=2322" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">38:42</a> | What are your views on the Nephilim: were they giants or corrupt human leaders? | He says he views the Nephilim through late Bronze Age priestly categories, not as giants or angel-human offspring, and sees them as powerful or feared humans connected to broken priestly vows. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=2703" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">45:03</a> | Do you have comments on the updated pre-publication interview transcription from Gershon Galil? | Yes. He says leaked photographs did not give him confidence in the proposed transcription and that early alphabetic inscriptions are especially difficult to read. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=2910" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:30</a> | How long did mortuary temples function after a king died? | He says they ran until their stored food and resources ran out; some lasted centuries, but a typical mortuary temple might last around 50 years after the king's death. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=3576" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">59:36</a> | Did Ramesses II marry his daughter Bint-Anath and have a child with her, and why would that have happened? | He says the marriage is real, but a child from that union is speculative; he interprets the arrangement as a ceremonial placeholder for the Great Royal Wife role. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=3918" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:18</a> | Is the Torah's use of older words an argument against the documentary hypothesis? | He says Egyptian loanwords support an early context, but he thinks literary arguments are also important because features used to split sources occur in unitary ancient Near Eastern texts. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=4315" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:11:55</a> | How can Jesus not knowing the day or hour in Matthew 24:36 and Mark 13:32 be reconciled with his divine nature? | He explains this through the kenosis of Christ: Jesus willfully set aside divine attributes such as omniscience during the incarnation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=4690" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:18:10</a> | What is the cultural context of the holy anointing oil and fragrances described around Exodus 29-30? | He reviews the ingredients, their ancient trade and use, and says the anointing oil was a fragrant, restricted temple recipe used for consecration. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XKgzzgmUBKg?t=5634" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:33:54</a> | Did Sumerians and Egyptians influence each other, and who invented writing first? | He says direct influence was limited and treats the writing question with caution, since the evidence is close and depends on the definition of writing. |
