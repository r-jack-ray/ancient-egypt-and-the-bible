| <a href="https://youtu.be/XcN_Xhr8bPM?t=2357" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39:17</a> | Were the extremely old ages in the Old Testament commonplace in other cultures of the period? | Other cultures have extreme ages, especially Mesopotamian king lists, but he thinks Old Testament ages may represent clan lifespans rather than individual lifespans. |

| <a href="https://youtu.be/XcN_Xhr8bPM?t=2890" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:10</a> | Did the average Egyptian family belong to something like a tribe or clan, and how important was lineage? | Yes, tribes or clans existed, and lineage mattered especially for upper classes, titles, inheritance, and land claims, though less for ordinary farmers. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/XcN_Xhr8bPM?t=3100" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">51:40</a> | Did Egypt have an equivalent of a Sabbath or day off during the week? | No. He says Egypt had a ten-day week with a half day off after nine and a half days, while Israel introduced the seven-day week and Sabbath. |

| <a href="https://youtu.be/XcN_Xhr8bPM?t=3398" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">56:38</a> | Why was Egypt so different from the Levant despite shared history? | He says Egypt conceived history as cyclical sameness, while Western and Levantine history emphasize change, so Egyptian history is often gleaned indirectly from religious texts. |
