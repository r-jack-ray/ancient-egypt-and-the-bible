| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=543" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:03</a> | What advice would he give amateur archaeologists who want to engage the material seriously without becoming professionals? | Read constantly. | <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=668" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">11:08</a> | Are readings like `Heights of David` and `Enclosure of Abraham` on Shoshenq I's Bubastite Portal real? | He said the `Heights of David` reading is fairly strong, while the `Enclosure of Abraham` reading is possible but less certain because the sign group may also refer to `stallions`. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=866" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:26</a> | Are there ancient Egyptian or Near Eastern parallels to Joseph's silver divination cup in Genesis 44:5? | Yes. He pointed to Tutankhamun's alabaster divination cup as a real Egyptian example, while noting silver vessels were rarer and more likely to be recycled. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=1033" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:13</a> | Is the idea that the tabernacle was a huge circular yurt or dome plausible? | No. He dismissed it as an engineer's fantasy that does not fit the biblical description or the need for portability. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=1192" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">19:52</a> | Are the genealogies in Matthew 1 and Luke 3 partly clan lists rather than only individual pedigrees? | Partly. He said the early portions that draw on Genesis should still be read largely in clan terms, while later portions move more toward individuals. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=1307" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">21:47</a> | How should the Jericho dating debate between about 1550 BC and about 1400 BC be reconciled, especially with the scarab of Amenhotep III? | He argued there is no real contradiction: a Middle Bronze destruction fits around 1550, later Late Bronze occupation existed, and newer work at Jericho undercuts the idea that the site was simply destroyed by Joshua around 1400. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=1657" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:37</a> | Could mainstream scholarship eventually accept a historical Exodus and conquest without accepting the supernatural claims? | He thinks the field is already moving that way, with more scholars willing to treat the events historically while bracketing miraculous interpretation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=1946" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:26</a> | Is there new archaeological evidence for Solomon or for Rehoboam's encounter with Shishak? | He said not much new has appeared recently, but he read the Shishak episode as pressure on Jerusalem that ended with a payoff rather than a full invasion of the city. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=2169" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">36:09</a> | If Adam's 930 years are really a clan lifespan, how should Genesis 5:5 be understood? | He argued the text reflects clan continuity rather than one individual's literal age, tying this to broader ancient Near Eastern ideas about lineage, memory, and the survival of a named house. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=2504" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41:44</a> | Should we expect to find Moses' name in hieroglyphs, and what might that name have looked like in Egyptian? | He said it is debated, but preferred a theophoric Egyptian background over the simpler `ms/msy` explanation, and thought an Egyptian form behind `Moshe` is plausible. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=2718" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">45:18</a> | What should be made of the so-called Samson seal from Tel Beth-Shemesh? | He said he knew of the claim but lacked enough provenance and excavation context to treat it confidently, so he would not build much on it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=2838" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:18</a> | Could someone say `I am` in ancient Hebrew without making a divine claim? | Yes. He said ordinary forms for `I am` existed, and the force of passages like John 8:58 depends on the specific divine verbal form being invoked. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=3078" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">51:18</a> | What does he think of David Rohl's claim that Joseph's tomb has been identified? | He rejected it as unconvincing, arguing the tomb count is overstated, the context is wrong, and the dating is about a century too early for Joseph. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=3344" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:44</a> | How does Israel's status as the chosen people relate to Messiah's coming and to Messianic believers? | He said Israel still has a special role in salvation history, but Jewish believers still come through grace and faith in Messiah like everyone else and can be especially effective witnesses. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=3904" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:04</a> | Did the Egyptians adopt foreign gods or identify them with their own deities? | Yes. He said Egypt regularly adopted foreign gods when they fit the wider polytheistic system, and later often merged foreign and Egyptian deities rather than claiming the foreign god had always been Egyptian. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=4200" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:10:00</a> | Is Leviticus 15:19 about menstrual blood polluting the land? | No. He read it as ritual language about normal and abnormal bodily states, not as a statement that menstrual blood pollutes the land. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=4531" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:31</a> | Was the star of Bethlehem present at Jesus' birth, and can it be used to date the birth? | He said the star belongs to the Magi episode after the birth, not the stable scene, so it does not directly give a birth date. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=4849" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:20:49</a> | What might life have been like for Moses growing up among Egyptians but not as one of them? | He pictured Moses growing up in the Ramesside household at Avaris, exposed both to elite Egyptian administration and to the surrounding Semitic-speaking local population. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=6841" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:54:01</a> | Roughly when in Horemheb's reign would Moses have fled Egypt? | He estimated around the twelfth to fourteenth regnal year of Horemheb, with Moses still comparatively young rather than already forty in a literal sense. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=8873" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:27:53</a> | How did new Egyptian dynasties legitimize themselves religiously? | He said they linked themselves to prestigious ancestors, poured wealth into temples, and at times used royal or prophetic texts to frame their rule as divinely warranted. |

| <a href="https://youtu.be/oz6vqfVDmQM?t=10762" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:59:22</a> | Is the chronology of the Israelite and Judahite kings basically settled, or does it still need major revision? | He said it may still need minor tweaks, but the overall framework is already very strong and does not need an overhaul. |
