|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=529" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:49</a> | What is a major factor driving people from Christianity into atheism? | Falk pointed especially to biblical illiteracy, bad context handling, fundamentalist rigidity, and untested discipleship. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=818" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">13:38</a> | Does the supposed divine-council inscription from Mount Ebal really date to the fifteenth century BCE, and would that support an early Exodus? | He was highly skeptical of the dating and the claims being made from it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=1131" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">18:51</a> | Roughly how old was Moses when he fled Egypt and when he returned? | He treated the biblical numbers as idiomatic and estimated Moses was probably in his late teens or twenties when he fled and around his late thirties when he returned. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=1268" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">21:08</a> | Could the broken skull of Ramesses II's firstborn show how the destroyer killed him? | Falk said no secure conclusion is possible because the identification of the skull itself is uncertain. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=1597" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">26:37</a> | Did Jesus point to an abomination of desolation like Antiochus IV's temple desecration? | Yes. Falk said Jesus alludes to both major and smaller temple abominations, with Antiochus as an important precedent. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=1889" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">31:29</a> | Could Ezekiel 40 describe a future third temple? | Possibly, but Falk said the passage remains disputed and cannot be settled neatly in a short answer. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=2140" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">35:40</a> | Do 2 Timothy 3 and John 10:35 teach that Scripture has no contradictions in the originals? | He said they support inerrancy in what Scripture affirms, while still allowing Scripture to truthfully record false statements made by characters. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=2574" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42:54</a> | Does Genesis 7:21-23 mean God completely wiped out humanity so there was no trace left? | He said the passage is clearly presenting a total wiping out of the pre-flood human order, even if readers still have to think carefully about what "blotted out" implies. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=8905" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:28:25</a> | In what year would he place Jesus' birth? | Around 4 BCE, in Falk's estimate. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=8992" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:29:52</a> | What is the best book on the Sea Peoples, and were they related to the Hyksos? | He said they were not related; the Hyksos were Semitic, while the Sea Peoples were Indo-European or mixed Aegean-Anatolian groups. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=9451" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:37:31</a> | Is there internal cultural evidence for the Exodus the way apologists argue there is for the New Testament? | Yes. Falk pointed to route names, Egyptian loan details, mourning customs, and many small Egyptian cultural markers in the text. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=9903" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:45:03</a> | What does it mean that Moses would be "as God" to Pharaoh? | It means Moses would stand before Pharaoh as a life-and-death divine agent, especially through the plagues and Passover judgments. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=10423" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:53:43</a> | Are the constellations in ancient Near Eastern literature basically the same as ours today, and how important were they? | Constellations mattered, but Falk said archaeoastronomy is far too imprecise to carry the chronological weight some people put on it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=10891" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:01:31</a> | Who were the Shasu, and what should we make of the "Shasu of Yhw" reference? | He said the Shasu were nomadic groups, but the Yhw reference is still too uncertain to prove that it names Yahweh as a deity there. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/VasTYoKMMe0?t=11210" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:06:50</a> | Is Ankhesenamun the best candidate for the Egyptian queen behind the Dakhamunzu affair? | Yes. Falk said she is the strongest fit for the Hittite correspondence usually associated with that episode. |
