| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=204" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:24</a> | Should Joshua 18:15 be translated as "the spring of Merneptah"? | Yes. Falk said the usual "spring of the waters of Nephtoah" reading is weak, while the Merneptah connection makes better linguistic sense. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=434" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:14</a> | Are there Egyptian texts that speak about plague-like events, and what does he think of Titus Kennedy's use of them? | He said Middle Kingdom texts do describe events resembling the plagues, but he rejected redating those texts to the 18th dynasty. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=863" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:23</a> | Was there a decline during the reign of Thutmose II? | No. Falk said Thutmose II belongs to the New Kingdom's rise rather than its decline, even if his reign had some unusual features. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=1063" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:43</a> | Was Genesis 1 written during the exile? | Probably not. He argued its core belongs much earlier, likely in the Late Bronze Age and possibly around the time of Moses. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=1266" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">21:06</a> | Why were Israelites forbidden to cut the sides of their heads, and were kosher rules partly anti-Egyptian? | He said many purity laws were mainly about making Israel a distinct people, sometimes over against Canaanite rather than Egyptian practice. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=1507" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">25:07</a> | What date does he favor for the Exodus, and does Amun-her-khepeshef's death help? | He favored about 1265 BCE and said the crown prince's death is helpful supporting evidence, though not a standalone proof. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=1680" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28:00</a> | Are Adam and Eve meant to parallel Mesopotamian myths, or are they unique? | He said Genesis is in conversation with those myths but is not copying them; it redirects them toward its own theological claims. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=2039" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33:59</a> | What does he think of Eric Cline's *1177 B.C.* and the Bronze Age collapse? | He accepted a collapse but said it was a long multi-stage process, not a single sharp event. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=2199" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">36:39</a> | If Merneptah was ruling by the time Israel was in Canaan, how does that fit the forty years in the wilderness? | He said the forty years are idiomatic rather than precise and that Israel would only need to be newly established in Canaan by Merneptah's early reign. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=2389" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">39:49</a> | What is the difference between Assyriology and Egyptology, and do the two fields interact much? | They are parallel but fairly siloed disciplines with different data sets and methods, though some cross-field dialogue does happen. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=2682" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">44:42</a> | Is the pre-Abraham material in Genesis inspired oral history, or direct factual revelation? | He treated Genesis as inspired scripture shaped through transmission, memory, and later updating for its audience rather than simple dictation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=3130" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">52:10</a> | Where is the creation text where Ptah speaks creation into existence, and does that make Ptah like Yahweh? | He pointed to the Memphis Theology on the Shabaka Stone and said the similarity is real but still far from identifying Ptah with Yahweh. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=3737" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:02:17</a> | What should a teenage Christian expect from studying biblical theology at a Christian college? | He said it can be rewarding, but students should choose carefully because restrictive or unhealthy school culture can damage faith instead of strengthening it. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=4220" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:10:20</a> | Was temple prostitution among the Canaanites considered sacred, or mostly transactional, and how widespread was it? | He leaned toward viewing it as transactional fundraising linked to temples rather than a sacred act in itself, and probably less pervasive than often imagined. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=4648" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:17:28</a> | Which ancient Egyptian king is his favorite to study? | He especially enjoys Ramesses II, though he also highlighted Tutankhamun and Thutmose III as fascinating figures. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=5018" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:23:38</a> | Which was Moses's greater "bowling achievement," the Red Sea split or Sinai? | He treated the Exodus as slightly more emphasized in scripture, while still calling both events foundational. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=5726" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:35:26</a> | How did Egyptians understand the soul and the whole human person? | He said Egyptian anthropology was multi-part, including body, ka, ba, heart, shadow, and name rather than a simple body-soul scheme. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/rccstKeVJoI?t=6252" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:44:12</a> | What DNA or genetic evidence most strongly proves the Bible's historicity? | He said DNA has been of limited use for biblical historicity because preservation is poor in Israel, though Egyptian mummification preserves more material. |
