# Questions in Livestream 22

Live Stream #22: The Catch-22 of Questions

Time links open the YouTube video at the relevant timestamp. The links use HTML anchors so GitHub can render them with `target="_blank"`.

| Time | Question | Short answer / answer direction |
|---:|---|---|
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=192" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:12</a> | Are Jannes and Jambres in 2 Timothy 3:8 real Egyptian New Kingdom names that could fit Ramesses's magicians? | He said no. He treated them as later Jewish legendary names added to otherwise unnamed biblical figures. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=444" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:24</a> | What are good resources on zooarchaeology in Egypt, Nubia, and the ancient Near East? | He especially recommended *The Mammals of Ancient Egypt* and suggested pairing it with more focused regional studies such as works on Abydos and the ivory trade. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=634" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">10:34</a> | Was ancient Egypt a kind of free-market economy? | Only partly. He described a barter-based market system inside a heavily constrained redistributive state economy. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=1010" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:50</a> | What caused the fallout between Ramesses VIII and the Theban priesthood? | He said the exact cause is unclear, but the broader hostility came from the priesthood's long resentment of the Ramesside line and preference for a Theban dynasty. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=1344" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:24</a> | What really happened in Nebuchadnezzar's move into Egypt, and is "conquest" the right word? | He said it was closer to a raid and temple-pillage than a full conquest. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=1689" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28:09</a> | How did Egyptians represent numbers in the time of Ramesses II? | He said Egyptian numerals were decimal, using separate signs for powers of ten and a working system for fractions. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=2049" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">34:09</a> | What kind of tombs would Jacob and Joseph likely have had in Egypt? | He leaned toward rock-cut tombs on higher ground near Avaris rather than wet low-lying necropolis burials. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=2620" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">43:40</a> | Can Joshua 17:16 and its "chariots of iron" language help date the conquest to the Late Bronze or early Iron Age? | Yes. He said it fits the overlap period when late Bronze and early Iron material culture meet. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=3174" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">52:54</a> | Are Genesis 1 to 11 best understood as mythohistory, and are they influenced by Egypt, Ugarit, and Babylonia? | In a qualified sense, yes. He described them as historical material interpreted through mythic language while engaging ancient Near Eastern backgrounds. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=3915" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:05:15</a> | What books would he recommend for teenagers starting apologetics? | He said to start with logic and church history rather than flashy apologetics titles. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=4359" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:12:39</a> | What does he think of people who say the Bible is poorly written and inconsistent? | He rejected that judgment and said the Bible is high literature for its world, with many problems arising from bad translation or poor reading. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=4689" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:18:09</a> | If early prophets did not speak against Asherah, does that mean they endorsed her? | No. He said silence is not endorsement and argued that some key evidence is often misread, especially the "his asherah" inscription. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=7421" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:23:41</a> | Are the seraphim basically dragons from various world mythologies? | He said no. He described them more narrowly as winged serpentine fire beings, not general dragon gods. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=7742" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:29:02</a> | What is his favorite geography book on the ancient Near East? | He recommended Barry Beitzel's *The New Moody Atlas of the Bible*. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=7981" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:33:01</a> | What does he think of Tel el-Hammam as Sodom and of the Shroud of Turin? | He was skeptical of both, calling the Sodom case especially weak and poorly supported. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=8440" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:40:40</a> | Could major Egyptian gods such as Ra, Horus, or Osiris originally have been human rulers? | Possibly. He said Egyptian tradition sometimes treats gods as earlier kings, and deification of humans did happen. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=8760" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:46:00</a> | Did the Egyptians have flood myths, and how do they compare with the biblical flood? | Not in the same way Mesopotamia did. He said the annual Nile flood reduced the need for a single-catastrophe myth, though Egyptian water and creation motifs still provide useful parallels. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/o5hu9f9P6Do?t=9044" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:50:44</a> | Is there an Egyptian ritual parallel that helps explain Noah's ark? | He suggested the ark can be compared to a temple box that sacralizes what is placed inside, purifying the old world into a new one. |
