| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=462" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">7:42</a> | Have you considered doing a tutorial series on archaeological methods or the historical method? | He says yes, including a possible project on archaeological fallacies, but he has no firm plan yet and wants to finish other book projects first. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=578" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">9:38</a> | Does the problem of evil go beyond Christianity, and do atheists face their own version of it? | He says yes: atheists still face a problem of defining evil without an all-good standard, even if their problem is not identical to the theistic one. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=843" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">14:03</a> | Is the Samaritan Pentateuch older and more accurate than the Masoretic Text? | He says it is probably older, perhaps by a few centuries, but older does not automatically mean more accurate; he still finds it very useful for textual criticism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=1060" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">17:40</a> | What is Yahweh's essence, how can it be one if there are other gods, and what do "essence" and "person" mean for God? | He gives only a brief answer: God's essence is his indivisible fullness or immensity, while the persons are distinct hypostases within that one divine essence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=1427" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">23:47</a> | Why do Genesis 10:19, Genesis 17:8, and Numbers 34 describe different borders for Canaan and the promised land? | He says later historical realities matter: Philistine control, the allotment to Abraham's wider descendants, and Israel's own disobedience all affect how the land is described and occupied. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=1998" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">33:18</a> | In Hebrew, why is `Elohim` pronounced that way instead of something like `Lahem`? | He thinks the vowel functions like a helper vowel, but says this is outside his strongest area and he cannot give a confident technical explanation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=2224" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">37:04</a> | Could Cyaxares II be Darius the Mede in Daniel 5:31? | He says the issue is highly controversial, but he is leaning more toward views that allow multiple royal names and sees some evidence in that direction. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=2429" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40:29</a> | How would you refute the full preterist claim that Isaiah 65:20 rules out a physical resurrection? | He rejects full preterism strongly and says Isaiah's prophetic language can be read as poetic or transitional, not as a denial of bodily resurrection. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=2939" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">48:59</a> | Could near-death experiences be supernatural, with consciousness partly entering the afterlife and then returning? | He says it is impossible to say with confidence, but he does not want to rule out the possibility that consciousness can leave the body and return. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=3182" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">53:02</a> | Have you heard of yacon syrup? | He had not heard of it before and briefly looks it up on stream, treating it as a new sweetener he may need to examine further. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=4137" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:08:57</a> | After finishing Joshua and moving into Judges, what should a reader keep in mind? | He says both books are arranged pedagogically from better to worse rather than simply chronologically, with tribal and judge sequences carrying theological meaning. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=4798" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:19:58</a> | What are your thoughts on baptismal regeneration, and is baptism salvific directly or indirectly? | He treats baptism as genuinely important and tied to salvation language, while signaling the topic is church-dividing and needs careful nuance. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=5253" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:27:33</a> | Can Revelation 12 be used to determine the birth date of Jesus, as Bullinger and Heiser suggest? | He says he has reservations and would need more specific evidence before accepting that reading. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=5834" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:37:14</a> | If you were in ancient Egypt and commanded to share Yahweh with Egyptians, what would be an effective evangelistic strategy? | He says it would be extremely difficult because one would be confronting an entire religious culture, not just making a simple individual appeal. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=7362" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:02:42</a> | Were noses removed from Egyptian statues as a reversal of the opening-of-the-mouth ritual? | He says nose damage usually comes from either vandalism or accident; in deliberate cases it can be a way of disfiguring the person for the afterlife. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=7702" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:08:22</a> | How did Joshua's men start fires when they burned cities? | He says flint would have been abundant and paired with straw, chaff, and other combustible material for ordinary ancient fire-making. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=7983" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:13:03</a> | What is the difference between a book and a codex? | He says a book is the literary work, while a codex is a bound-page format; all codices are books, but not all ancient books were codices. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=8263" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:17:43</a> | If parapsychology is often considered pseudoscience, why do you take any of it seriously? | He distinguishes between parapsychology as a discipline, which he treats cautiously, and paranormal phenomena, which he thinks may be real. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=8584" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:23:04</a> | Which commentaries on Leviticus and Ezekiel would you recommend? | Instead of naming a shortlist, he recommends commentary-guide books such as those by Tremper Longman and D.A. Carson so readers can evaluate commentaries more broadly. |

| <a href="https://youtu.be/tDAmbiFJLAU?t=9303" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:35:03</a> | If the census number 603,550 in Numbers 1 only works by rethinking terms like clans and hundreds, how do you make the census work? | He says the usual literal number is not geographically tenable, suspects military or clan terminology is involved, and thinks the underlying system is still not fully understood. |
