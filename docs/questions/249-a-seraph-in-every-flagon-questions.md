| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=1733" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">28:53</a> | Can physical pursuits like exercise be acts of worship comparable to study, prayer, or fasting? | Maybe. | <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=1937" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:17</a> | What are the strengths and weaknesses of the arguments about dating the book of Daniel? | He treated the issue as less decisive than many assume, saying both early and late dating proposals have arguments, and he was not heavily dogmatic about the result. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=2448" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">40:48</a> | Are there extra-biblical accounts of Necho merely wanting to pass by while Josiah interfered? | No direct extra-biblical account of that encounter. He treated Josiah's move as a bad political and military decision in the biblical setting. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=3741" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:02:21</a> | How long was Joseph vizier: only the famine years, or the rest of his life? | He said the Bible does not tell us. Joseph could have served a shorter or longer term, and the office was always subject to royal replacement. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=3840" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:04:00</a> | Is the Tower of Babel unrealistic given language history, or is the story plausible? | He said readers should not overread the text. In his view the passage is plausible when read as the text intends, not as a modern linguistics treatise. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=6053" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:40:53</a> | Why does Genesis 24:59 mention Rebekah's nurse going with her? | He explained the nurse as a governess-like figure responsible for the young woman's protection, training, and social supervision during the transition to marriage. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=7274" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:01:14</a> | How much confidence should we have in near-death experiences? | Very little. He treated NDEs as experiences during the dying process, not reliable windows into the afterlife. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=7721" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:08:41</a> | What does God's oneness mean in relation to the Trinity? | He summarized it as `one what, three who`: one divine essence with three distinct persons, even though the inner mechanics remain beyond full human explanation. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=8613" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:23:33</a> | How should we understand Akhenaten's monotheism, and should it be equated with Yahweh worship? | He rejected the identification, treating Atenism as its own Egyptian phenomenon rather than a true parallel to biblical monotheism. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=9312" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:35:12</a> | Are the serpent in Genesis, the adversary in Job, and Satan in the New Testament the same figure? | Maybe, but he would not make the identification absolute in every detail. He allowed continuity while leaving room for textual nuance. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=9515" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">2:38:35</a> | What do you say to someone who fears eternity, even heaven, because living forever sounds frightening? | He said eternal life must be understood as blessed life with God, not endless boredom; the fear usually comes from imagining a distorted version of eternity. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=11126" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:05:26</a> | Will there be human languages in heaven? | He said yes, appealing to Revelation's many-peoples imagery and suggesting there is no reason to think redeemed human diversity disappears. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/jwt2Gc_6L0E?t=13092" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">3:38:12</a> | What is the most important practical advice for someone thinking about adopting rabbits? | His clearest advice was to spay or neuter them and not treat rabbit care casually, because good care requires more structure than many people expect. |
