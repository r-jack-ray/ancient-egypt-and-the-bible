| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=105" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:45</a> | Which king created the Great Sphinx: Khafre or Djedefre? | He corrects himself and says the evidence points strongly to Khafre. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=309" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">5:09</a> | Can the Song of Moses in Exodus 15 be used as evidence because it is old, and are other Torah sections especially old? | He says many Pentateuchal source texts look very old, especially Genesis/Joseph and Exodus material, though he has not studied Exodus 15 closely enough to make a specific claim. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=493" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8:13</a> | What are your thoughts on the Admonitions of Ipuwer? | He treats it as a Middle Kingdom prophetic text that reflects Egyptian fears later echoed by exodus plague imagery. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=752" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">12:32</a> | Are you familiar with the work of Algis Uzdavinys, and what are your thoughts? | He was not familiar with the name and did not comment on the work. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=814" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">13:34</a> | Does Joseph's Egyptian name date him, and would a seven-year famine leave scientific evidence? | Joseph's name is genuinely Egyptian but not narrowly datable; a famine would be hard to detect because Egypt often lived near subsistence. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=1001" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">16:41</a> | Why is Egyptian presence not mentioned much in Judges, Samuel, and Kings? | Kings does mention later Egyptian campaigns; Judges and Samuel occur when Egypt was largely too internally troubled to campaign in the Levant. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=1370" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">22:50</a> | During famine, how could Egypt support migrants, and were they farming or buying surplus food? | Egypt's delta and stored grain could support migrants; foreigners could herd flocks and trade for grain rather than receive food freely. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=1670" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">27:50</a> | Could KV5 be connected to the death of the firstborn of Egypt? | He says yes, in that it was built for Ramesses II's children and likely began after the death of his firstborn. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=1798" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">29:58</a> | How long did it usually take to build a pyramid or a temple? | It varied widely: simple temples could take a year or two, major temple complexes centuries, and pyramids from a few years to around 30 years. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=1962" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">32:42</a> | Where did the Israelites get cattle for tabernacle sacrifices if God sent quail for food? | He says they had cattle, sheep, and goats from Egypt, but sacrifices were costly and could reduce their food resources. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=2259" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">37:39</a> | Where did the Israelites get expensive tabernacle materials after leaving Egypt in a hurry? | He says most precious materials came from Egyptian neighbors as unpaid wages or compensation, while wood and some materials came from the Sinai environment. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=2505" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">41:45</a> | Do you know anything about the 10,000-year-old basket found in Israel? | He knows little beyond that it appears to be a Neolithic woven basket and awaits more publication. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=2578" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">42:58</a> | How do you respond to scholars who say Israelites were originally polytheistic and monotheism came later? | He largely agrees Israel struggled with polytheism, but places the beginning of monotheism at Sinai and its full lesson after the exile. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=2878" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">47:58</a> | Is there a connection between Apophis and Leviathan or Lotan? | He does not see a strong connection and treats Apophis and Leviathan as distinct mythological figures. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=3017" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">50:17</a> | Is Nimrod Sargon of Akkad? | He is not convinced, says the idea is old, and thinks the issue needs closer study. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=3235" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">53:55</a> | Could the cemetery at Avaris have begun after the exodus because of deaths from the plagues? | It is possible and suggestive, but agricultural leveling makes it impossible to prove. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=3336" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">55:36</a> | Does the Merneptah Stele saying Israel was laid waste hurt the biblical narrative? | No. He says the wording is poetic and the stele actually supports Israel being in the land as a people group alongside the Hurrians. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=3641" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:00:41</a> | Could the pillar of cloud and fire have a natural explanation? | He says historians cannot confirm or deny miracles, but Exodus often includes natural components, so natural weather and divine action need not be mutually exclusive. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=3980" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:06:20</a> | If Amraphel in Genesis 14 is Hammurabi, why has that view been abandoned? | He says the abandonment is often ideological or chronology-driven, and he still finds the Hammurabi connection etymologically plausible. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4252" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:10:52</a> | Which Egyptian creation myths would Israelites have encountered, and where can one read more? | He says they would have been exposed broadly to Egyptian and other regional creation traditions, and suggests general works on Egyptian gods and mythology. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4433" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:13:53</a> | Did Moses write Genesis 1-11, or was it a later Babylonian exile addition? | He rejects a Babylonian exile addition and thinks Genesis was written during the Sinai period, possibly by scribes under Moses' authority. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4535" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:15:35</a> | Where can artists find accurate information for ancient-world clothing, tools, and objects? | He recommends archaeological reports, museum collections, some consultant-backed visual books, Petrie on tools, and Geoffrey Killen on furniture. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4740" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:19:00</a> | Was reincarnation ever part of Gnostic belief? | No. He says Gnosticism saw flesh negatively, so reincarnation would be contrary to its aim of liberation from the body. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4809" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:20:09</a> | Is there evidence that Midian was in the Sinai Peninsula besides Moses going west? | Direct evidence is scarce, but biblical and ecological evidence make Midianite pastoral presence in Sinai plausible. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=4947" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:22:27</a> | Why did God want to kill Moses in the bridegroom of blood incident? | Moses had failed to circumcise his children and was therefore neglecting the Abrahamic covenant he was about to represent. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=5180" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:26:20</a> | Is there Egyptian mythology about giants that could contextualize biblical giants in Canaan? | Not really. Egyptian art can portray gods or kings as oversized, but he does not see a myth of human giants. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=5255" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:27:35</a> | Who ruled Egypt when the king was away on campaign? | The vizier handled day-to-day administration and would run the kingdom when the king was away. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=5372" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:29:32</a> | Were the ten plagues judgments on ten specific Egyptian gods? | No. He says the text specifically frames the tenth plague as judgment on Egypt's gods and power structure, not each plague as targeting one god. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=5658" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:34:18</a> | Were there cultural differences between Upper and Lower Egypt? | Some, especially diet and environment, but preservation in Lower Egypt is difficult and the differences were not huge. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=5805" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:36:45</a> | Could Moses have been addressing the grand vizier rather than Pharaoh? | He rejects this; the vizier was not called Pharaoh and lacked the authority shown in the exodus narrative. |
| <a href="https://youtu.be/6w6NTLN3two?t=6130" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">1:42:10</a> | Have archaeologists found the land of Goshen in Egypt? | Yes in the sense that Goshen is the region around Avaris and Pi-Ramesses, whose locations are known. |
